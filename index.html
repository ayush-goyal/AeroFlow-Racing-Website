Who We Are

We are AeroFlow Racing and our team is made up of five Wheeler Magnet juniors (left to right): Ayush Goyal, Poojan Mehta, Arul Gupta, Arya Mevada, and Shivam Patel. Each of us bring a unique skillset to the table, however one common attribute among all of us is the ability to collaborate. We intend to reach many new heights including first in the national competition as well as substantial results at the world competition through dedication and determination. We are one team, with one goal, with one passion. We are AeroFlow Racing.

The Challenge

A global STEM competition

  • 20,000,000 Students
  • 40 Countries
  • 1 Experience

F1 in Schools is a worldwide competition consisting of several teams made up of kids aged 11-18. In this vastly competitive league, teams of 3-6 develop cars utilizing CAD and other related tools. However, the complexity of what we do reaches beyond mere engineering and into fields of business/marketing, graphic design, and possibly most importantly if considering real-world application, team management and cooperation. Then, each of these skill sets developed throughout the team process is put to the test at regional, national, and world level competitions. As a result, F1 in Schools has created a system which effectively develops and enhances students’ learning experience through their interests as well as by teaching necessary real-world situations/applications.

Past Results

The AeroFlow Racing team just returned from a very successful 2017 F1 in Schools National Finals in Austin, Texas. The competition lasted 3 days where we had the opportunity to witness several other teams and students across the Nation, compete against those teams, give a verbal presentation to a table of judges, meet with colleges, and much more. The competition encompassed several different judging criteria and we were just one team out of fifteen total competing.

Although we did not qualify for the World Competition, we received three various awards (Best Verbal Presentation, Best Marketing and Sponsorship Strategy, and Most Innovative Team) out of ten possible, and more importantly, learned an immense amount about where we can improve to ensure our ability to make the World Competition in 2018.
